The Merit of a Challenge

June 14, 2015

When looking at the list of values that we were provided, the one that stood out to me the most was challenge. I have always been a competetive person, and my greatest opponent has always been myself; I am always competing with myself to get better and better at whatever it is that I am doing. Over the years, I have found that the best way to do this is by challenging myself. Whether it be academics, athletics, or music, I always preferred surrounding myself with people who were better than me. I would never get better at anything if I was never challenged, so what better way to do that than work with people who are smarter, faster, or more talented than me. If I am challenged by others I am in constant competition with myself to get better, and I'm sure the same will happen with coding.

I think this value will help me especially at DBC. In my last blog I wrote about the stereotype threat that I feel as being a beginner in the coding world, and I think holding up my value of using challenges as a growing experience will help combat this very effectively. Whenever I start to feel discouraged becuase I don't know enough or get frustrated becuase I am not immediately understanding something, I will just remember that all of these challenges will be a learning experience that will allow me to grow as both a programmer and a person.
